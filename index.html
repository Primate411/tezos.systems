What are Bakers?

Bakers are validators who secure the Tezos network. They create new blocks, validate transactions, and earn rewards for their work. Anyone with enough XTZ can become a baker or delegate their tokens to one.

How Tezos Consensus Works

Tezos uses Tenderbake, a consensus mechanism inspired by classical BFT (Byzantine Fault Tolerant) algorithms. It provides fast finality—once a block is confirmed, it's permanent and can't be reversed.

Key features:

  • Deterministic finality: Transactions are final in about 30 seconds, with no risk of chain reorganizations
  • Energy efficient: Uses Proof of Stake instead of energy-intensive mining
  • Liquid staking: You can delegate your XTZ to bakers without locking them up
  • Self-amendment: The protocol can upgrade itself through on-chain voting

Tenderbake was introduced in the Ithaca upgrade (2022), replacing the previous Emmy* consensus. It significantly improved transaction finality and network reliability.

Understanding Tezos Economics

Tezos uses Adaptive Issuance—a smart system that automatically adjusts how many new tokens are created based on network participation. This keeps the economy balanced and sustainable.

How it works:

  • Dynamic rewards: When more people stake, issuance decreases. When fewer stake, it increases—incentivizing participation
  • Target staking ratio: The system aims for around 50% of XTZ to be staked for optimal security
  • No fixed cap: Unlike Bitcoin, Tezos has controlled inflation to continuously reward network participants
  • Burns & fees: Transaction fees and some protocol operations burn XTZ, partially offsetting new issuance

Adaptive Issuance was introduced in the Paris protocol (June 2024), replacing the previous fixed ~5% annual issuance with a smarter, market-responsive system.
